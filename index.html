Introduction to Cyber Security

Protecting the digital frontier in an era of evolving threats.

What is Cyber Security?

Cyber security is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These cyberattacks are usually aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information; extorting money from users via ransomware; or interrupting normal business processes.

The Need

In today's connected world, everyone benefits from advanced cyber-defense programs. At an individual level, a cybersecurity attack can result in everything from identity theft, to extortion attempts, to the loss of important data like family photos.

Malware & Its Types

🚫

Adware

Software that generates revenue for its developer by automatically generating online advertisements in the user interface.

🕵️

Spyware

Malicious software that enters a user's computer, gathers data from the device and user, and sends it to third parties without consent.

🦠

Virus

A type of program that, when executed, replicates itself by modifying other computer programs and inserting its own code.

🪱

Worms

Standalone malware computer program that replicates itself in order to spread to other computers.

🐴

Trojan-horse

Any malware which misleads users of its true intent. Often looks like legitimate software but performs malicious actions.

😨

Scareware

A form of malware which uses social engineering to cause shock, anxiety, or the perception of a threat in order to manipulate users into buying unwanted software.

🌐

Browser Hacking

Malicious software that changes a browser's settings without the user's permission and redirects the user to websites they did not intend to visit.

Cyber Crime & Its Kinds

Cyber Stalking

The use of the Internet or other electronic means to stalk or harass an individual, group, or organization.

Child Pornography

The visual depiction of children in sexually explicit situations, a grave violation of human rights and a serious crime.

Software Piracy & IPRs

Unauthorized copying, distribution, or use of software; and crimes related to Intellectual Property Rights.

Cyber Terrorism

The use of computer-based weapons to disrupt infrastructure or conduct attacks that result in violence or significant financial loss.
