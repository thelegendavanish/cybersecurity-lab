What is Data Diddling?

✏️

Early Stage Attack

It involves changing data before or during entry into a computer system, often by a trusted insider.

🪄

Subtle Changes

Unlike a catastrophic hack, diddling makes small changes (like changing an account number or salary) that go unnoticed.

🛡️

Integrity Threat

This attack directly attacks the **Integrity** part of the CIA Triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability).



Defending Against Diddling

📠

Hash Verification

Using checksums or cryptographic hashes to verify that data hasn't changed since it was sent.

👁️

Dual Control

Requiring two people to approve any sensitive data entry—one to enter and one to verify.

📜

Audit Logging

Maintaining a detailed, tamper-proof log of who entered what data and when.
