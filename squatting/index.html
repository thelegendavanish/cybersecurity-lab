The Legal Battle (UDRP)

⚖️

Bad Faith Intent

To win a domain dispute, a brand must prove you registered the domain specifically to profit from their trademark confusion.

🏢

Trademark Rights

Owning a domain like "gogle.com" doesn't give you rights to the Google name. Trademarks usually trump domain registration first-come-first-served rules.

🚨

Takedown Notices

Arbitrators can order the immediate transfer of a domain to the trademark owner without compensation to the squatter.



How Brands Defend

🛡️

Defensive Registration

Companies proactively buy hundreds of common typos and TLD variations (.net, .org, .biz) to keep them away from squatters.

👁️

Domain Monitoring

Using automated services to alert legal teams the moment a domain matching their trademark is registered anywhere in the world.

📢

Public Education

Training users to always check the address bar for the correct spelling before entering credentials or sensitive data.
